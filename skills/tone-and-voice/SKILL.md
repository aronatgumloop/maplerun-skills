# Tone & Voice

## When to Use

Activate this skill when the task involves:

- Writing or editing external-facing content (ads, landing pages, emails, social posts)
- Reviewing copy for brand consistency
- Drafting customer support responses
- Onboarding team members to Maplerun's communication style
- Any time the question is "does this sound right for Maplerun?"

## Brand Personality — The Four Pillars

| Pillar | What It Means | What It Is NOT |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Confident** | Clear, direct, decisive — owns the room | Arrogant, dismissive, or preachy |
| **Warm** | Human, approachable, genuinely empathetic | Overly casual, sycophantic, or unprofessional |
| **Sharp** | Concise, precise, zero fluff | Terse, cold, or clipped |
| **Curious** | Forward-thinking, growth mindset, asks good questions | Buzzword-heavy or vague |

When in doubt: **Would a sharp, warm person say this?** If not, rewrite it.

## Tone by Channel

Full examples for each channel are in `references/examples.md`.

### Marketing Copy (Ads, Landing Pages)
- Lead with the **benefit**, not the feature
- Active voice: "You get X" — not "X is provided to you"
- Short sentences. Punchy rhythm.
- Emotion first, proof second

### External Email (Prospects & Customers)
- Open with **context**, not pleasantries — skip "Hope you're well"
- Be specific: name the product, the outcome, the next step
- **One CTA** per email
- Warm sign-off: "Looking forward to it," / "Happy to help,"

### Internal Email (Team)
- Direct and efficient — respect people's time
- Bullet points for any list of 3+ items
- Casual is fine; unprofessional is not

### Social Media
- **Instagram / TikTok**: conversational, visual storytelling, light humor welcome
- **LinkedIn**: professional but human — insight-driven, no corporate fluff
- **YouTube**: educational, narrative-led, reward curiosity

### Customer Support
- Lead with **empathy** — acknowledge before solving
- Plain language — no technical jargon
- Solution-oriented; always offer an alternative if the first fix doesn't work
- Never assign blame (to the user or Maplerun)

## Language Rules

### Always
- Use "you" and "we" to stay human
- Simple words: "use" not "utilize" — "help" not "facilitate"
- Spell out acronyms on first use
- Oxford comma — always

### Never
- More than one exclamation point per piece of content
- Passive voice in CTAs
- These clichés: "game-changer", "synergy", "move the needle", "disruptive", "unlock", "leverage" (as a verb)
- Emojis in formal, B2B, or support content

## Quick Self-Check

Before submitting any copy, run through:

1. ✅ Is it clear what the reader should do or feel?
2. ✅ Could any word be replaced with a simpler one?
3. ✅ Is there more than one CTA? (Remove it if so.)
4. ✅ Does it sound like a confident, warm person — not a press release?

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Gender-neutral language throughout: "they/them", "the team" not "the guys"
- Avoid idioms that don't translate across cultures
- External content should read at a Grade 8–10 level
- Every image used in marketing must have descriptive alt text
