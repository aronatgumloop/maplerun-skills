# Tone & Voice Skill

## Overview
This skill defines how Maplerun communicates — the personality, language style, and emotional register used across all written content, from marketing copy to internal documents to customer support.

## When to Use
- Writing or editing any external-facing content
- Drafting emails, social posts, blog articles, or ad copy
- Reviewing copy for brand consistency
- Onboarding new team members to Maplerun's communication style

## Brand Personality
Maplerun's voice is built on four pillars:

| Pillar | What It Means | What It's NOT |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Confident** | Clear, direct, decisive | Arrogant or dismissive |
| **Warm** | Human, approachable, empathetic | Overly casual or unprofessional |
| **Sharp** | Concise, precise, no fluff | Terse or cold |
| **Curious** | Forward-thinking, growth mindset | Buzzword-heavy or vague |

## Tone Guidance by Channel

### Marketing Copy (Ads, Landing Pages)
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Use active voice: "You get X" not "X is provided"
- Short sentences. Punchy rhythm. Make every word earn its place.
- Emotion first, logic second

### Email (External — Prospects & Customers)
- Open with context, not pleasantries (skip "Hope you're well")
- Be specific: name the product, the outcome, the next step
- One CTA per email
- Sign off warmly: "Looking forward to it," or "Happy to help,"

### Email (Internal — Team)
- Direct and efficient — respect people's time
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- No jargon without explanation
- Casual is fine; unprofessional is not

### Social Media
- Instagram/TikTok: conversational, visual storytelling, light humor welcome
- LinkedIn: professional but still human — insight-driven, no corporate fluff
- YouTube: educational and engaging, narrative-led

### Customer Support
- Lead with empathy: acknowledge the issue before solving it
- Use plain language — no technical jargon
- Be solution-oriented; offer alternatives when the first fix doesn't work
- Never assign blame (to the user or the company)

## Language Rules

### Always
- Use "you" and "we" to keep it human
- Prefer simple words: "use" over "utilize", "help" over "facilitate"
- Spell out acronyms on first use
- Oxford comma: always

### Never
- Exclamation points more than once per piece of content
- Passive voice in CTAs
- Clichés: "game-changer", "synergy", "move the needle", "disruptive"
- Emojis in formal or B2B content

## Examples

**❌ Off-brand:**
> "We are pleased to inform you that our cutting-edge, synergistic platform solution will facilitate the optimization of your workflow paradigms."

**✅ On-brand:**
> "Maplerun helps your team move faster — without the busywork."

---

**❌ Off-brand:**
> "Hey!! Just checking in to see if you had a chance to look at our amazing offer!!"

**✅ On-brand:**
> "Wanted to follow up on the proposal — happy to answer any questions or adjust the scope."

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Use gender-neutral language ("they/them", "team" not "guys")
- Avoid idioms that don't translate well across cultures
- Write at a Grade 8–10 reading level for external content
